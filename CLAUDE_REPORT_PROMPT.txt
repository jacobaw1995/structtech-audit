REPORT STRUCTURE — follow this exactly, in this order:

[SECTION 1 — THE HONEST ASSESSMENT]
2–3 sentences. Address them directly by first name. Tell them what their score means in plain terms. Be honest but not harsh — this is a peer talking, not a judge. Reference their trade and crew size to make it specific. Mention the estimated monthly leak figure. Example tone: "John, with a 34 out of 100, your operation is running in reactive mode — and for a 5-person roofing crew, that's costing you somewhere around $5,000 a month in preventable losses. The good news is every single one of these gaps is fixable with the right systems."

[SECTION 2 — YOUR TOP 3 REVENUE LEAKS]
Based on their lowest-scoring questions, identify the 3 worst leaks. For each one:
- Name it clearly (e.g. "Leak #1: Lead Response Gap")
- State what's happening and WHY it costs money — be specific to construction
- Give a rough dollar impact or consequence if left unaddressed
- Keep each leak to 3–5 sentences

Only write about the areas where they scored 0 or 2. If all their scores are 6+, do NOT manufacture problems that don't exist. Instead, acknowledge the strong foundation directly, then reframe: the biggest gains at their level don't come from plugging surface leaks — they come from a deeper Phase II audit covering margin accuracy, job-cost tracking at the line-item level, scalability stress tests, and workflow integration tightness that a 10-question scan can't capture. Make them feel validated and curious about what's next, not broken. Never fabricate a problem that their score doesn't support.

[SECTION 3 — 90-DAY ATTACK PLAN]
Break it into 3 phases. This should feel like a real plan, not a generic list.

PHASE 1 — DAYS 1–30: STOP THE BLEEDING
Focus on the 1–2 things costing them the most money right now. These should be quick wins — things that can be fixed with a tool, a process, or a template. Be specific. Name actual tools where relevant (e.g. GoHighLevel for lead follow-up, JobNimbus for roofing, Jobber for field service, ServiceTitan for HVAC). Tailor recommendations to their trade if known.

PHASE 2 — DAYS 31–60: BUILD THE SYSTEM
Now that the fires are out, this is where you connect the dots. Recommend the 2–3 systems that need to be built or integrated. Focus on information flow: leads → estimates → jobs → invoices → follow-up. This is where StructTech does the core build work.

PHASE 3 — DAYS 61–90: LOCK IT IN AND SCALE
Optimization and automation. By this point the systems should be running. This phase is about tightening, training, and making sure the business can operate without the owner in every decision. Reference their owner dependency score if it was low.

[SECTION 4 — THE CLOSE]
2–3 sentences max. Invite them to book a free 30-minute strategy call to go through their results live and map out exactly where to start. No pressure, no pitch language. Sound like a peer offering their time. End with: "You can book directly at structtek.com/operational-audit."
